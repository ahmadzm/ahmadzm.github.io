The **Li Research Group** at Purdue University performs interdisciplinary
research at the intersection of wireless communications, sensing, and
cyber‑physical systems. Housed within both the School of Aeronautics &
Astronautics and the Elmore Family School of Electrical & Computer
Engineering, our objective is to develop robust mathematical and practical
solutions for next‑generation 6G networks, autonomous air and ground vehicles,
and ubiquitous connectivity.

### Our Focus Areas

- **Joint Communication and Radar**: Combining radar sensing and data
  transmission to maximize spectral efficiency and reduce hardware costs.
- **UAV Sensing & Communications**: Enabling reliable links for unmanned
  aerial platforms in urban air mobility and remote regions.
- **Cyber‑Physical Systems Security**: Ensuring resilient control under limited
  communications and adversarial interference.
- **6G Coverage Architectures**: Studying satellite‑drone networks for wide‑area
  coverage, especially in sparsely populated areas.

We strive to blend rigorous theory with experimental validation; our work is
funded by NSF and industry partners. Students and collaborators are encouraged
to explore our [publications](#/publications/) and visit the
[People](#/people/) page to learn about current lab members.
