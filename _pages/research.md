The Li Research Group tackles several interrelated technical challenges:

### Joint Communication and Radar

We develop algorithms and hardware for transmitting and receiving signals that
simultaneously carry data and provide sensing information. This integration
reduces spectrum usage and hardware costs for applications such as urban air
mobility and autonomous vehicles.

### UAV Sensing & Communications

Our team studies reliable networking for unmanned aerial vehicles operating in
both urban and remote environments. Projects include adaptive beamforming,
coverage optimization, and coexistence with 5G/6G infrastructure.

### Cyber-Physical Systems Security

We analyze the vulnerabilities of control systems when communication channels
are constrained or compromised. Our work aims to design resilient control and
estimation schemes that maintain safety under adversarial interference.

### Coverage Architectures for 6G

As 6G targets ubiquitous connectivity, we investigate satellite–drone networks
and other architectures that prioritize coverage over throughput, particularly
in sparsely populated regions.

Many of these themes overlap; we often pursue unified mathematical frameworks
that guide both theory and experimental validation. See our
[publications](/publications/) for technical details and [projects](/projects/)
for ongoing work.
